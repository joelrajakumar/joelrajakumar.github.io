I am a PhD candidate in computer science at the University of Maryland, advised by Yi-Kai Liu and Michael Gullans, affliliated with the Joint Center for Quantum Information and Computer Science (QuICS) and the Institute for Robust Quantum Simulation (RQS).

My work focuses on understanding what quantum devices can compute and when their outputs can (or cannot) be mimicked classically. I am especially interested in models of current device architectures, such as noisy, random, and/or shallow circuits, and in quantum algorithms proposed for the next generation of devices. A broader motiviation of mine is to probe aspects of many-body physics through a computational complexity lens.
