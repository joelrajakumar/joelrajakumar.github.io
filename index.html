I am a PhD candidate in computer science at the University of Maryland, advised by Yi-Kai Liu and Michael Gullans, affliliated with the Joint Center for Quantum Information and Computer Science (QuICS) and the Institute for Robust Quantum Simulation (RQS).

My work focuses on how noise, structure, and measurements affect what quantum circuits can compute—and when their outputs can (or cannot) be mimicked classically. I am especially interested in random circuit models, shallow architectures, variational algorithms, and sampling problems connected to quantum advantage.
